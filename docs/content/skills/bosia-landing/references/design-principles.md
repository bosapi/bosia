# Landing — principles

## Lineage

- open-design `skills/frontend-design` — production UI, typographic-first.
- open-design `skills/ui-skills` — registry composition.

## Why editorial theme

Landings are typography-led: the hero is words, not chrome. The `editorial` theme favors serif-display headlines, generous whitespace, restrained color — pushes the eye to copy first.

## Section rhythm

Above the fold = hero. One promise.
Below = proof. Features, then social proof, then a restated CTA.

The page is a funnel: each section narrows attention toward one action.

## Typography ladder

- `h1`: display, 48–64px desktop, 36–40px mobile.
- `h2`: 28–36px, sections.
- Body: 16–18px, line-height ~1.6.

Use `ui/typography` to get this for free.

## Common failures

- Two competing primary CTAs above the fold.
- Feature cards that paragraph instead of one-line.
- Logo row that's louder than the hero copy.
- Hero with no concrete benefit (only abstractions).
