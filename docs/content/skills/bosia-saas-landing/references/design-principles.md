# SaaS landing — principles

## Lineage

- open-design `skills/frontend-design` — section rhythm, hierarchy.
- open-design `skills/marketing-psychology` — social proof, anchor framing.
- open-design `skills/ui-skills` — registry composition.

## Section funnel

Each section narrows the user toward one action.

1. **Hero** — capture attention with a promise.
2. **Logo row** — "real people use this".
3. **Features** — "here's how it delivers the promise".
4. **Deep feature** — "the one thing that makes it different".
5. **Testimonials** — "people like you said yes".
6. **Pricing tease** — "this is the price of yes".
7. **CTA** — "say yes".

Skip steps only if your audience is sophisticated enough not to need them.

## Why testimonials must be real

The single fastest way to lose trust is a generic, photoless testimonial. People recognize them instantly. If you don't have real ones yet, omit the section.

## Two CTAs in the hero

- Primary: the conversion verb ("Get started", "Start free").
- Secondary: the de-risking verb ("Read the docs", "See it in action").

Same size, different visual weight. Never two identical primary buttons.

## Logo row discipline

- Grayscale logos at ~60–70% opacity.
- All same height.
- No more than 6–8 logos.
- No outdated / churned logos.
